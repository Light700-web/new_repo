--- ### 🗼 1. Tokyo Isn’t Technically a City Tokyo is actually a **metropolis (prefecture)** made up of 23 special wards, each functioning like its own mini-city. --- ### 🚆 2. The Train System Is Insanely Punctual Tokyo trains are so accurate that being **even 1 minute late** can come with an official apology. Delays are a big deal. --- ### 🧍‍♂️ 3. The World’s Busiest Crossing Is in Tokyo Shibuya Crossing sees **thousands of people crossing at once**—it’s one of the most famous intersections on Earth. --- ### 🍜 4. Tokyo Has the Most Michelin-Star Restaurants in the World More than Paris or New York. You can eat some of the **best food on the planet**, from luxury dining to cheap street meals. --- ### 🏢 5. Space Is So Limited, People Rent Tiny Apartments Some apartments are extremely small—just enough for a bed and essentials. Tokyo living can be very compact. --- ### 🤖 6. Vending Machines Are Everywhere There are millions of vending machines, and they don’t just sell drinks—you can find: * Hot meals * Umbrellas * Ice cream * Even random everyday items --- ### 🌸 7. Cherry Blossom Season Is a Big Deal When sakura (cherry blossoms) bloom, people gather for **hanami (flower viewing)** picnics—it’s a major cultural event. --- ### 👮 8. It’s One of the Safest Cities in the World Despite being huge, Tokyo has **very low crime rates**. People even leave belongings unattended in public. --- ### 🎮 9. It’s a Global Hub for Anime & Gaming Areas like **Akihabara** are heaven for anime, manga, and gaming fans. --- ### 🧼 10. Clean Streets, Few Trash Bins Public trash cans are rare, yet the city stays very clean. People usually carry their trash home. --- ### 🍣 11. Sushi Isn’t Just Fancy Food There In Tokyo, sushi can be: * Super cheap and casual * Or extremely high-end It’s everyday food, not just luxury.
